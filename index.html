Your Workouts

Five focused 45-minute sessions per week. Each designed for home dumbbells, optimized for muscle growth.

Chest + Triceps
Duration: 45 min
Exercises: 7
Focus: Push mechanics
Start light. Control every rep. Chest work done right beats heavy sloppy reps every time.
01
Dumbbell Flat Press
4 × 10–1260s rest
Primary
Lie on the floor. Lower dumbbells slowly (3 sec down). Drive up explosively. Feel the chest squeeze at top.
02
Dumbbell Incline Press
3 × 10–1260s rest
Primary
Use a pillow stack or bag to create 30–45° incline. Targets upper chest. Critical for a full chest look.
03
Slow Negative Push-Ups
3 × max reps45s rest
Volume
3 second descent, explode up. When you hit 20+ reps easily, elevate feet on a chair for greater difficulty.
04
Dumbbell Chest Fly (Floor)
3 × 12–1545s rest
Volume
Wide arc motion. Never lock out elbows. Light weight, deep stretch. Builds the inner chest line.
05
Overhead Tricep Extension
3 × 1245s rest
Isolation
Both hands on one dumbbell. Elbows pointed forward. Full stretch at bottom. Squeezes the long head.
06
Tricep Kickbacks
3 × 12–1545s rest
Isolation
Hinge forward at hip. Keep upper arm parallel to floor. Extend fully. Pause 1 sec at lockout.
07
Diamond Push-Ups
2 × max30s rest
Finisher
Hands form a diamond shape. Elbows stay close to body. Brutal tricep burnout to end the session.
📈
Progressive Overload TargetEach Monday: aim for 1 more rep OR 1–2 kg heavier on the flat press vs last week. Write it down.
Back + Biceps
Duration: 45 min
Exercises: 7
Focus: Pull mechanics
Pull with your elbows, not your hands. Imagine your hands as hooks — let your back do the work.
01
Two-Arm Bent-Over Row
4 × 10–1260s rest
Primary
Hinge forward 45°, back flat, drive elbows toward hips. Squeeze shoulder blades together at top for 1 second.
02
Single-Arm Dumbbell Row
3 × 12 each60s rest
Primary
Support on knee and hand. Full range of motion — arm fully extended, elbow past torso. Unilateral for balance.
03
Romanian Deadlift
3 × 10–1275s rest
Primary
Dumbbells at thighs. Hinge at hips, feel hamstring stretch. Drive hips forward to stand. Back stays flat always.
04
Reverse Fly
3 × 1545s rest
Volume
Hinge forward. Arms out to sides like wings. Light weight — this targets the rear delts and upper back. Don't ego lift.
05
Alternating Bicep Curl
3 × 12 each45s rest
Isolation
Supinate wrist at top (rotate palm up). Full extension at bottom. No swinging — elbows stay pinned to sides.
06
Hammer Curl
3 × 12 each45s rest
Isolation
Thumbs-up grip throughout. Builds brachialis (outer bicep thickness) and forearm. Great for arm size.
07
Concentration Curl
2 × 12 each30s rest
Finisher
Seated, elbow braced on inner thigh. Slowest curl you can do. Peak contraction, hold 2 seconds at top.
📈
Progressive Overload TargetEach Tuesday: add a rep to your bent-over row OR increase weight. Rows are your primary back builder.

Supplements

Food first, always. Supplements fill specific gaps — nothing more. These four are all you need.

Must Have
Creatine
5g daily · in water or juice
Single most proven supplement in exercise science. Increases strength, muscle volume, and performance. Take every day regardless of training. No loading phase needed. Results visible in 3–4 weeks.
Must Have
Vitamin D3
2000–4000 IU daily · with K2
You work indoors 12 hours a day. Deficiency is near-certain. Low D3 crushes testosterone, immunity, and mood. Take D3 + K2 together. This is not optional for someone with your schedule.
Highly Useful
Whey Protein
1 scoop post-workout · only if needed
Use only if you genuinely cannot hit 160g protein from food. Post-workout shake is ideal timing. Not magic — it's just convenient food. 30g per scoop. Buy unflavoured or chocolate. Avoid cheap brands with fillers.
Highly Useful
Omega-3
1g EPA+DHA daily · with food
Reduces muscle soreness and systemic inflammation from training. Improves brain function (useful for your work day) and joint health. Take with your largest meal. Look for at least 1g combined EPA+DHA per serving.

The Rules

Eight rules that separate results from wasted effort. Follow all eight. There are no shortcuts.

01
Progressive overload
Add weight or reps every single week
Your body adapts to the same stimulus and stops responding. Every week, aim for 1 more rep or 1–2 kg heavier on at least one exercise per session. Keep a physical notebook. This single habit is responsible for 80% of your results.
02
Protein priority
Hit 160g protein no matter what
For skinny fat, protein is the single most critical dietary variable. It builds muscle AND helps lose fat simultaneously. If you miss calories occasionally, that's manageable. If you miss protein consistently, muscle growth stops completely. Pack eggs and paneer everywhere.
03
Sleep
10 PM sleep, 5 AM wake — protect this window
Testosterone and growth hormone peak during deep sleep stages 3–4. Missing even one hour of sleep reduces next-day training performance by up to 30% and increases cortisol (the muscle-destroying hormone). No screen after 9:30 PM. This is training, not rest.
04
Meal preparation
Cook Sunday, pack every night
With a 7–7 job, you will 100% fail on nutrition unless food is prepared in advance. Sunday: cook in bulk (chicken, dal, boil eggs). Every night before bed: pack tomorrow's complete tiffin with all snacks. This is what makes the plan actually work during the week.
05
Technique first
Weeks 1–4 are form training, not strength training
As a beginner, injury is your #1 enemy — it will end your progress for weeks or months. Spend the first month with lighter dumbbells perfecting form. Record yourself. For pressing movements: 2 sec up, 3 sec down. Never use momentum. Bad form wasted the exercise and causes long-term damage.
06
Tracking
Measure everything, weekly
Take a photo every 4 weeks, same lighting, same time, same pose. Track body weight every Sunday morning (after toilet, before eating). Track all lifts in a notebook — weight used and reps completed. You will not feel like you're changing for 6–8 weeks. Photos and numbers will prove you are.
07
Skinny fat strategy
Lean bulk only — 200–300 cal surplus
Skinny fat means low muscle + excess body fat. A dirty bulk (eating everything) will only make you fatter. A lean bulk of 2,700–2,900 cal daily builds muscle while fat stays flat or decreases. This is called body recomposition. Be patient — visible changes take 3–6 months. Do not panic and slash calories.
08
Lifestyle
No alcohol — it actively destroys gains
Even 2 drinks reduces testosterone by 23% for the following 24 hours and disrupts the deep sleep stages where growth hormone is released. You are waking at 4:45 AM, training hard, and eating carefully — alcohol directly undoes all of it. Occasional (monthly) is manageable. Weekly drinking is incompatible with this program.
